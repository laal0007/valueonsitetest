User Inputs

Quality/Performance

Quality/Performance is the value for any given Line Item as perceived by the user.


List Price

List Price for each viable purchase option or Line Item that satisfies a minimum MANDATORY REQUIREMENT as defined by the user.


Base

Base, highlighted in red, represents the Line Item against which all other Line Items are compared. It may or may not be the lowest Price Line Item. The user may have ostensibly decided on a purchase and can populate the Table with higher and lower Price Line Items to see if the intended purchase seems reasonable. There may also be occasions where a Line Item that does not offer the "best overall value" but the user still has a preference for it. In this case we will set the preferred item as the base to determine if the metrics can justify acquisition of the item in question.


Calculated Values

Relative Value

Relative Value is the difference in dollar terms between the List Price and an internally calculated Value Price (VP). A positive amount represents a Value Price greater than the List Price which is a desirable outcome with the opposite being true. The magnitude of the difference determines the relative value for each option.

Analysis

When gathering data for market research it is often worthwhile to confine the search to one particular vendor. After completion of market analysis and the preferred product/service is identified this baseline can then be compared to other options in the marketplace to determine if a superior product exists.

Overall changes to the Price Weight will have a greater impact than changes to the metric for R. With this in mind our suggestion is that metrics for R should be established in the first instance with various modeling outcomes being a function of altering the PW.

The nature of product/service to be acquired is also a consideration. In general terms they can be divided into consumable versus capital expenditures. For example a vacation is a consumable, one and done, while a new lawn tractor would be a capital expenditure. Thus price might be more heavily weighted in the context of a consumable as opposed to a capital purchase where there is an extended period of time over which a price premium can be amortized.

The user should not be reluctant to use partial points when establishing metrics for R as we have done in the foregoing Value Analysis Table. There will often be instances where the differences in quality/performance as perceived by the user are not easily distinguishable. Moreover, competing vendors may offer incentives in terms of delivery dates, shipping costs, loyalty programs, etc that appeal to the user. That being the case the use of partial points can provide the granularity necessary to facilitate a meaningful comparison analysis.
